Mitigating Representation Bias Caused by Missing Pixels in Methane Plume Detection.

Julia Wąsala1,2, Bram Maasakkers2, Ilse Aben2, Rochelle Schneider3, Holger Hoos4, Mitra Baratchi1,
1Leiden Institute of Advanced Computer Science (LIACS), 2SRON Space Research Organisation Netherlands, 3Phi-lab, ESA-ESRIN, 4Chair of AI Methodology, RWTH Aachen
Accepted at the Workshop on Machine Learning for Earth Observation in conjunction with the ECML/PKDD 2025
Code

Missing not at random (MNAR) data, such as missing pixels in satellite image data products, can lead to representation bias in deep learning. We mitigate this bias on two levels: on the image-level by filling missing pixels, and on a dataset-level by removing the association between missing pixels and the target variable.
